7. How will you show the presence of water vapour in the air?  [2]

Ans:  THis is an experiment to show the Presence of water vapour in the Air

Water vapour is contained in the atmosphere, which can be easily depicted using the following experiment :

Materials– A glass beaker and ice cubes

Procedure –  The following procedure can be performed to show the presence of water vapour in air :

1. Take the glass beaker and dry it from outside.

2. Place the ice cubes in the beaker.

3. Leave the ice along with the beaker untouched for some time.

Observations –

Water droplets collect on the outside of the glass beaker. .This proves that water vapour is present in the air.

9. What is the importance of the water cycle?   [3]

Ans: The water cycle is an extremely important process because it enables the availability of water for all living organisms and regulates weather patterns on our planet. If water didn’t naturally recycle itself, we would run out of clean water, which is essential to life.Freshwater seems abundant, but when accounting for all the water on Earth, it's in limited supply. Just three percent of the water on our planet is freshwater. A majority of this water, about two percent of the world total, is contained in glaciers and ice sheets or stored below ground. The remaining one percent is found in lakes, rivers and wetland areas or transported through the atmosphere in the form of water vapor, clouds and precipitation.

11. What are the factors affecting the rate of evaporation? [2]

Ans: The key factors that affect the rate of evaporation of a liquid are:

  • Temperature: The greater the temperature of the liquid and its surroundings, the faster the rate of evaporation.
  • Surface area occupied by the liquid: Since evaporation is a surface phenomenon, the greater the surface area occupied by the liquid, the quicker it undergoes evaporation.
  • Humidity of the surroundings: The greater the humidity of the atmosphere surrounding the water, the slower the rate of evaporation.
  • Air circulation or wind speed: The presence of a breeze or another source of air circulation directly contributes towards the rate of evaporation.
